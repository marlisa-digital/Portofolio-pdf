My story

How I got here

"I'm not a marketer who learned data. I'm an analyst who found her way into marketing — and that makes all the difference."

My career started in accounting, then 12 years across the insurance industry — validating 1,000+ claims per month, building reporting systems from scratch, all the way to internal audit and anti-money laundering compliance. At every stage, one thing was always true: data was everywhere, but rarely actually used.

In August 2025, I was laid off. Not an ending — a turning point.

I went freelance, joined the Virtualeaf team, and found myself in digital marketing. That's when everything clicked. Funnel audits, social media analytics, KPI dashboards — I don't just read metrics. I read workflows, operational patterns, and systemic gaps. Because I've lived inside them for 12 years.

That's what makes my approach different: I don't just know the numbers are dropping — I know why the system behind them isn't working.

FAQ

Questions you might be wondering

Honest answers before you decide to work together.

How long does an audit take?

For Social Media Audits and Website/Funnel Audits, typically 3–5 business days from when all access and briefs are received. For more complex dashboards or strategies, it can be 7–14 days. I always confirm the timeline upfront before we start.

What tool access do you need?

It depends on the service. For website/funnel audits, I need read-only GA4 access and optionally Google Search Console. For social media audits, access to your account's Insights/Analytics is sufficient. I never ask for full admin access — read-only is enough for analysis.

Is this suitable for a brand-new business?

Absolutely. New businesses actually benefit most from an early audit — before spending budget on the wrong strategy. I have experience working with businesses starting from zero (like Bliss Bakery) all the way to established operations.

Is there a results guarantee?

I can't guarantee specific conversion numbers since many factors are outside my control (product, pricing, market). What I do guarantee: an honest, specific, and immediately actionable audit report — not generic advice. If you're not satisfied, I'll revise until the output is truly useful.
